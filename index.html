How to Convert CGPA to GPA:

A Guide for Converting a 10-Point CGPA to a 4-Point GPA !

Grading systems exhibit variations across institutions and countries, highlighting the need for methods to convert between different scales. One frequently encountered conversion is from CGPA to GPA. This page seeks to elucidate the conversion process and the utilization of a CGPA to GPA calculation, how to convert CGPA to GPA, underlining its importance for students making transitions between educational systems.


What is GPA?

GPA, or Grade Point Average, is a numerical representation of a student’s overall performance over a semester or time. It is normally assessed on a scale comprising five grades: A, B, C, D, and F, in which A represents the highest achievement, and F indicates a failing grade. American universities commonly use a 4-point GPA scale. To compute your GPA on this scale, follow this formula: GPA= Number of Courses Taken/Total Sum of Grade Points In this calculation, sum all the grade factors earned after which divide by the entire wide variety of courses taken. Maintaining a strong GPA can substantially advantage international students to scholarships and admission to prestigious universities.

What is CGPA?

CGPA, or Cumulative Grade Point Average, provides an overview of a student’s overall academic performance. Achieving a high GPA contributes to a correspondingly high CGPA score The CGPA is determined by calculating the average grade points earned across all semesters and then dividing by the total number of semesters CGPA=Total Number of Semesters/Average Grade Points of All Semesters
